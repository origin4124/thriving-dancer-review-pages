Picking Up Choreography After Years of Salsa

4 min readMedia versions planned

A practical look at how previous dance experience can speed up learning new choreography.

The usable angle here is that dancers often learn faster when new choreography lands on top of years of prior movement practice.

Dancers who have experience with previous forms of dance, such as salsa for the past 10-plus years, can quickly pick up new choreography. This is because their bodies are already accustomed to recognizing patterns and repeating movements, which makes it easier to learn new routines.

For example, if you've done some dance before picking up the basics of a new choreography, wasn't it too bad that you mostly got it? That's because your body has already developed a pattern recognition system that helps you understand and execute new movements more efficiently.

The source also mentions siloing in the United States / New York dance context. This means that dancers often focus on their own styles or genres, which can limit their exposure to different forms of movement. However, by drawing from previous experiences and learning from various sources, dancers can expand their skill set and improve their overall performance.

So if you're looking for a quick reset around how dancers learn faster once pattern recognition is already in the body, this article provides practical advice on how to make the most of your existing dance experience. By leveraging your past movements and building upon them, you can pick up new choreography more quickly and easily.
