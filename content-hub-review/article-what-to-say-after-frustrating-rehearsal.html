What to say after a frustrating rehearsal

5 min readMedia versions planned

A parent-and-dancer support piece with language that lowers the temperature after hard corrections or emotional studio days.

After a rough rehearsal, the instinct is often to fix the feeling immediately. But most dancers do not need a lecture right away. They need steadiness.

Good support language usually sounds like calm observation before advice: that looked heavy, I can see why that stayed with you, do you want to talk through it now or later?

The goal is not to erase the hard day. It is to keep the hard day from turning into a bigger story about talent, worth, or whether the dancer belongs.
