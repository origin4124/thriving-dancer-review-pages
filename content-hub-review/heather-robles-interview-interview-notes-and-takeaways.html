Heather Robles interview — interview notes and takeaways

4 min readMedia versions planned

We very often in the United States tend to be in a silo, especially where I live in New York. It's very important to the company that I'm dancing with right now, Suzanne Pana-Marinco-Dance. So to step outside of that and to connect with people around the world, it expands my sense of the world of my community and communities very important to me.

Heather Robles talks about building an independent dance life that spans choreography, performance, travel, and embodied care work rather than staying confined to one narrow lane.
